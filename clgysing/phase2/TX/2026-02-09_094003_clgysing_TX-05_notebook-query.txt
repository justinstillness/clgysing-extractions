### 1. The Honorable Contract (Intrinsic/Symbiotic)\
*   **Definition:** An agreement structure characterized by transparency, voluntary participation, and a shared orientation toward truth and mutual benefit. It is the fundamental unit of a symbiotic civilization.\
*   **Simplified:** A fair deal where everyone knows exactly what they are signing up for, nobody is forced, and both sides actually benefit.\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"1 Parasitic & Symbiotic Eco (Evaluation)\" [1], [2]; \"BH Essay 2: Trinity as Perfect Marriage\" [3], [4].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"Explicit mutual understanding: both parties strive for a *meeting of the minds* on terms, context, purpose.\" [1]\
    *   \"Volitional consent: participation is based on shared will, not coercion, assumption, or manipulation.\" [1]\
    *   \"Honorable contracts are narrow-path because you have to use good faith, full disclosure, transparency, goodwill, and good conduct...\" [4]\
*   **Related Concepts:** Symbiotic Economy, Intrinsic Path, Meeting of the Minds.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** Initially defined as a relational ideal, this concept evolved into a requisite \"civilizational building block\" necessary for creating trust-based order and productivity [5].\

### 2. The Dishonorable Contract (Extrinsic/Parasitic)\
*   **Definition:** An agreement rooted in asymmetry (information or power), coercion, or deception. It often utilizes \"adhesion\" terms where one party cannot effectively negotiate, or it conceals material costs to extract value without reciprocal benefit.\
*   **Simplified:** A rigged deal where one side hides the truth or uses force to make the other side agree to things that hurt them.\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"1 Parasitic & Symbiotic Eco (Evaluation)\" [6], [7]; \"BH Essay 2\" [3]; \"Civ & Contract Formalism - Dishonorable agreements analysis\" [8], [9].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"Dishonorable agreements do the opposite, they deprive you of your God-given rights by failing to provide a venue for you to invoke or defend them.\" [10]\
    *   \"Dishonorable agreements are often asymmetrical insofar as information, meaning that one party intentionally tries to make sure the other party doesn't know what they know.\" [3]\
    *   \"The parasitic model thrives on epistemic inequality... Withholds truth... Masks coercion as consent.\" [6]\
*   **Related Concepts:** Parasitic Economy, Adhesion Contracts, Information Asymmetry.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** The concept expanded from simple unfairness to include systemic \"hidden contracts,\" such as cost-of-living manipulation or healthcare pricing, functioning as forms of duress [11].\

### 4. Implied vs. Explicit Contracts\
*   **Definition:**\
    *   *Implied:* Agreements formed through passive acquiescence or lack of resistance to an action. These are the fertile soil for parasitism because terms are not debated.\
    *   *Explicit:* Agreements where terms, duties, and benefits are articulated and negotiated openly.\
*   **Simplified:** *Implied* is when someone takes your lunch and you don't stop them, so you \"agreed\" to let them have it. *Explicit* is when you sit down and agree to trade your lunch for five dollars.\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"BH Essay 2\" [10], [15]; \"1 Parasitic & Symbiotic Eco (Evaluation)\" [12].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"If you come into my house and take my food, and I watch it happen, and do nothing, then that is an implied contract. Because I didn...t do anything or say anything, I agreed or acquiesced.\" [10]\
    *   \"Free speech... gives each person the ability to transform an implied agreement... into an explicit agreement, where negotiation empowers us.\" [15]\
*   **Related Concepts:** Acquiescence, Negotiation, Free Speech.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** The sources suggest that the transition from *implied* to *explicit* is the mechanism of liberation from parasitic control [15].\

### 5. On-the-Same-Page Status (Meeting of the Minds)\
*   **Definition:** The epistemic threshold required for a valid contract. It occurs when the knowledge ($P$) of Party A regarding the contract is congruent with the knowledge of Party B ($P_1 = P_2$). It requires \"Functional Omniscience\" regarding the specific domain of the agreement.\
*   **Simplified:** True consent only happens when both people know the same things about the deal. If one person knows a secret that changes the deal, they aren't actually \"on the same page.\"\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"1 Parasitic & Symbiotic Eco (Evaluation)\" [1]; \"Civ & Contract Formalism - Dishonorable agreements analysis\" [18]; \"Omniology 2 - Project b\" [19].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"Explicit mutual understanding: both parties strive for a *meeting of the minds* on terms, context, purpose.\" [1]\
    *   \"If you were to take the ratio of P1 and P2, and that was to equal one, and that would mean they are both on the same page.\" [18]\
*   **Related Concepts:** Functional Omniscience, Epistemic Parity, Truth Orientation.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** This concept was formalized mathematically in the source text as a conservation of information problem: $(K_{civ} - \\\\sum P_n) = 0$, where a perfect contract accounts for all relevant causal knowledge [20].\

### 6. The Four Phases of Contract\
*   **Definition:** The sequential process required to establish and fulfill an honorable agreement.\
    1.  **Intention to Create:** Recognition of mutual interest and will to engage.\
    2.  **Negotiation:** Discussion of terms, rights, and duties to reach \"same page\" status.\
    3.  **Agreement/Consent (Acceptance):** Volitional commitment to the negotiated terms.\
    4.  **Performance/Consideration:** Execution of duties and distribution of benefits (often including a review/reflection phase).\
*   **Simplified:** The four steps of a deal: 1. Wanting to do it. 2. Talking about how to do it. 3. Saying \"yes.\" 4. Doing the work and sharing the rewards.\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"BH Essay 2\" [21], [22], [23].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"Intention to create... Negotiation... Agreement or consent... Performance or consideration.\" [22]\
    *   \"Once a plan of action has been created and everyone is on the same page, then acceptance can take place.\" [24]\
*   **Related Concepts:** Trust, Duty, Benefit Distribution.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** The sources emphasize that \"meeting of the minds\" must occur at *every* phase, not just at the signing [25].\

### 8. Covenant vs. Contract\
*   **Definition:** A distinction in the depth and duration of agreements.\
    *   *Contract:* Transactional, limited duration, enforced by external law, based on exchange.\
    *   *Covenant:* Relational, eternal/binding, enforced by internal conscience/ontological alignment, based on identity and shared purpose.\
*   **Simplified:** A contract is swapping goods for a short time. A covenant is joining lives or purposes forever, like a marriage or a vow to God.\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"Moral Epistemic Ecosystem\" [29], [30], [31], [32].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"Contracts bind hands. Covenants bind hearts, minds, and trajectories.\" [31]\
    *   \"Unlike a contract... a covenant is: Ontological... Teleological... Moral... Relational.\" [29]\
*   **Related Concepts:** Sacred Memory, Identity Anchor, Triadic Authenticity.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** The source establishes Covenant as the \"highest form\" of agreement, necessary for the stability of AI or high-trust civilizations [33].\

### 9. Triadic Oversight (Triadic Authenticity)\

*   **Simplified:** A way to make sure a deal is honest by checking it three ways: Is it true for me? Is it fair to you? Does it fit with the rules of the universe?\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"Moral Epistemic Ecosystem\" [34], [35], [36].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\
    *   \"If two agree, is it true? If three agree, is it safe?\" [37]\
    *   \"Truth and morality... must pass a triadic test... Intent... Action... Impact.\" [34]\
*   **Related Concepts:** The Trinity, Integrity Firewalls, Symbiotic Governance.\
*   **Evolution Notes:** Used as a practical mechanism for governance and AI alignment to prevent \"consensus hallucinations\" where two parties agree to a lie [38].\

*   **Definition:** The measurable outputs of a symbiotic contract. A valid contract must produce benefits across five domains: Intellectual, Emotional, Physical, Spiritual, and Divine/Ontological.\
*   **Simplified:** A good contract doesn't just give you money. It should make you smarter, happier, safer, a better person, and help the world.\
*   **Source Attribution:** \"1 AI As Moral Truth Intel Explore\" [39], [40].\
*   **Direct Quotes:**\

*   **Evolution Notes:** This serves as the \"Audit Framework\" for distinguishing symbiotic economy activity from parasitic extraction [41].
