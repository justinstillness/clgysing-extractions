PART VI: MAIL & EXTERNAL COMMUNICATIONS\
================================================================================\
================================================================================\
\
This part addresses AI integration with email and external communications.\
Where earlier parts focus on AI-assisted internal work, Part VI addresses\
workflows where AI output enters external channels...email to clients,\
communications to stakeholders, and other outward-facing contexts.\
\
External communications carry higher stakes than internal work. Errors\
reach external parties. Tone mismatches damage relationships. The\
verification discipline from Section 8 applies with amplified importance.\
\
================================================================================\
SECTION 15: EMAIL INTEGRATION\
================================================================================\
\
Email remains a primary business communication channel. AI integration\
with email workflows requires attention to both input processing (using\
email as AI input) and output processing (using AI to generate email\
content).\
\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
15.1 Email-to-AI Workflow\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
\
Email content frequently requires AI processing: summarization, response\
drafting, information extraction, or action item identification. This\
subsection addresses patterns for bringing email content into AI workflows.\
\
Email Ingestion\
Email enters AI context through several paths:\
\
Direct paste. For individual emails, copying content directly into the\
chat is often sufficient. Strip headers unless they're relevant to the\
task.\
\
Batch processing. For multiple emails, compile into a structured\
document with clear email boundaries.\
\
Attachment handling. Email attachments should be processed separately\
and provided to AI in appropriate format (see Section 11 for file type\
guidance). Don't assume AI can access attachments from email paste.\
\
Privacy Considerations\
Email content often contains sensitive information. Apply redaction protocols\
before processing and consider data retention policies.\
\
Common Email Processing Tasks\
Summarization, Response draftin< 2026/02/12 23:47:55.000763328  length=8192 from=26543 to=34734
g, Information extraction, Thread synthesis,\
and Sentiment analysis.\
\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
15.2 AI-to-Email Workflow\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
\
AI-generated email content requires careful handling before sending.\
Email reaches external parties; errors and tone mismatches have\
relational consequences.\
\
Generation Protocol\
When generating email content:\
- Provide relationship context, not just message content.\
- Provide the prior thread if responding.\
- Be explicit about constraints and goals.\
\
Verification Requirements\
All AI-generated email content requires review before sending:\
- Accuracy check (factual claims)\
- Tone calibration (read aloud)\
- Commitment review (unintended promises)\
- Recipient perspective check\
\
Email-Specific Failure Modes\
Register mismatch, Over-length, Hollow politeness, Missing human judgment.\

================================================================================\
SECTION 16: EXTERNAL COMMUNICATIONS\
================================================================================\
\
External communications extend beyond email to include client\
deliverables, stakeholder updates, public-facing content, and any\
material that represents you or your organization to outside parties.\
\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
16.1 Client Communications\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
\
Client communications include: formal deliverables, progress reports,\
meeting materials, and ongoing correspondence.\
\
Quality Thresholds\
Client-facing materials should meet higher quality standards than\
internal work:\
- Elevated verification (consider Tier 2 validation)\
- Style consistency\
- Error intolerance\
\
Approval Workflows\
For significant client communications, implement approval workflow:\
Draft generation ... Internal review ... Quality verification ... Approval gate ... Send.\
\
AI should not send client communications directly. AI generates\
content; human approves and sends. This gate is non-negotiable.\
\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
16.2 Stakeholder Communications\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
\
Stakeholder communications address parties with interest in your work\
beyond direct clients: partners, investors, regulators, media, public.\
\
Risk Management\
- Public record potential: Assume anything written may become public.\
- Commitment scrutiny: Be precise about what is promised vs. anticipated.\
- Regulatory awareness: Verify compliance implications.\
\
Consistency Management\
Maintain messaging alignment and temporal consistency across stakeholder\
communications.\

17.2 Glossary\
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------\
\
ALIGNMENT TURN\
A mandatory exchange at the start of every continuation session where the AI\
generates a checklist of scope, materials, and workflow for user verification\
before substantive work begins.\
\
BOOTSTRAP\
A document capturing project state, decisions, and next actions for\
handoff to a new AI session. The primary vehicle for multi-session continuity.\
\
BRANCH STARTUP FILE\
A specialized context packet used to initialize a new branch workstream.\
Contains inherited context, branch scope, and mini-rooting questions.\
\
CEREMONY IS CONTINUITY\
The philosophy that formal artifact creation (bootstraps, logs) is not\
bureaucracy but the mechanical necessity that enables memory in stateless\
AI systems.\
\
CONNECTOR TRANSPARENCY\
The protocol requiring AI to narrate all MCP tool interactions (queries,\
results) in the visible transcript to prevent context invisibility.\
\
CROSS-BRANCH AWARENESS DOCUMENT\
A living document stored in Project Knowledge that lists active branches,\
objectives, and coordination needs, ensuring sessions maintain visibility\
of parallel work.\
\
EXHAUSTIVE QUERYING\
A notebook retrieval technique involving iterative cycles of query, summary,\
and gap-checking until information saturation is reached.\
\
FINDINGS REPORT\
A synthesis document capturing empirical discoveries organized by topic (F-##)\
rather than chronology.\
\
HEADROOM OPPORTUNISM\
The strategic practice of utilizing remaining token capacity after primary\
objectives are met to complete secondary tasks without compromising closeout.\
\
HUSK VS. COCONUT\
The filtering principle used at phase transitions to separate ephemeral\
conversational content (husk) from essential crystallized knowledge (coconut)\
to be carried forward.\
\
INSTRUCTION CHAIN INTERPRETATION\
The rule that multi-step user instructions are interpreted as sequential\
with alignment checkpoints, not as batch commands.\
\
NEW CHAT NOTEPAD\
A user-side scratchpad for staging session initiation prompts, ensuring\
all context and reminders are ready before opening the AI interface.\
\
PHASE PROTOCOL\
A phase-stable governance document defining how the AI should behave\
during a specific project phase. Composed of three layers: Framework,\
Methodology, and Project Context.\
\
PROJECT HANDLE\
A short, consistent alphanumeric tag (3-10 characters) used to identify\
a project across files, folders, and notebooks (e.g., GOV-AIPM).\
\
RESPONSE INDEX\
A persistent, project-wide sequential numbering system ([1], [2]...)\
for substantive AI responses, enabling precise retrieval from transcripts.\
\
ROOTING (PHASE 0)\
The formal process of establishing a project's foundation (Project Guide,\
Phase Protocol) before execution begins.\
\
SESSION FIDELITY REPORT (SFR)\
A structured assessment embedded in the Session Log evaluating objective\
achievement, phase progress, and handoff readiness.\
\
SINE WAVE MODEL\
A mental model for project rhythm alternating between high-output execution\
peaks and consolidation/planning zero-crossings (transitions).\
\
THREE-TIER DOCUMENTATION\
A documentation architecture separating Architecture References (stable),\
Component References (operational), and Changelogs (temporal).\
